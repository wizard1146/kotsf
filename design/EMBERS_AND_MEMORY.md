# The Embers & the Memory of Magic

*The load-bearing origin myth of KOTSF. This is the spine everything else hangs
from — the intro card, the Codex, the Forgetting economy, and the mid/late-game
Ember arc. Marked **canon** (Forgotten Runes) vs **ours** (invented in the lore's
silences) so we never over-claim.*

---

## The spine, in one breath

Once the **Sacred Flame** was tended whole in the **Secret Tower**, and — though no
one knew it — its true function was to **permissively grant the world its memory of
magic**. When the Tower's **Keepers vanished** and its enemies gathered, the coven
that kept it **broke the Flame into Embers** and scattered them to hidden hearths so
the whole could not be seized. **Runehold is one such hearth.** With the Flame no
longer whole or tended, the world began to **forget** — and only as spells unravelled
did anyone begin to grasp why.

## The three linked ideas

### 1. The Embers (a diaspora, not one relic)
- The original Tower coven **shattered the Flame** and fled, each carrying a shard.
- Every **Ember** is the burning heart of a hearth (like Runehold's hearth-rune).
- The rest are **out there**: kept, lost, gone cold, corrupted, or in enemy hands.
- **Hook:** recovering other Embers is a **mid/late-game arc** — expeditions on the
  Map, rival hearths to win/trade with/take from, reuniting shards as the road to
  **re-lighting the Tower**. Turns "keep your hearth alive" into "find the others
  first." Count is deliberately **unknown** (design can size the arc later).

### 2. The Flame's true function — discovered, not known
- The Flame **grants the world its memory of magic.** Keepers thought they tended a
  holy fire; they were tending the world's memory.
- Split and dimmed, that memory **leaks out** → the **Forgetting** (mechanically our
  decaying **Lore** meter; thematically: spells unravel, Wizards fail).
- **Stays hidden at the start**; revealed through play. Ties naturally to **Blue /
  the Forgotten Athenaeum** as the cult chasing the truth.

### 3. Tomes & Stories (the reveal, through spectacular failure)
- The truth surfaces piecemeal via recovered **Tomes and Stories** — accounts of
  **great Wizards attempting epic feats and failing catastrophically** as the memory
  drained. Dark, cautionary, sometimes tragicomic.
- **Hook:** a **content type** — collectible lore fragments surfaced by scenes, Lore
  actions, exploration, or the Athenaeum. Each fills the **Codex**; some grant Lore
  or unlock Ember-quests.

## How the appointment reads
You are the **appointed Archon** — but **who named you, and why, is half-forgotten**
(the Forgetting ate it). This is the chosen framing: the appointment itself is a
mystery hook the Codex/scenes can pay off. (Alternatives considered: last Warden /
dying predecessor / the coven raised you — kept in reserve.)

## Open threads (deliberately unresolved)
- **Who were the Tower's enemies** who gathered when the Keepers left? Left vague for
  now — opportunists at minimum; may connect to the deeper **Shadowing** (Wardens
  left → the Tower went dark → the Quantum Shadow seeped in). Not committed.
- **How many Embers**, and are any already lost/corrupted? Unknown by design.
- **Does reuniting all Embers re-light the Tower**, or is more needed (the Keepers
  themselves, the Wardens, an Infinity-Veil rune-quest)? Open.

## Canon vs. ours
- **Canon:** the Sacred Flame, the Secret Tower, Forgotten Souls, the seven colour
  cults/schools, the Forgotten Athenaeum, Wizards & Warriors.
- **Ours:** breaking the Flame into **Embers**; the Flame as the world's **memory of
  magic**; **Runehold** as an Ember-hearth; the **appointed Archon**; **Tomes &
  Stories** as the discovery vehicle; the whole **Shadowing** layer.
